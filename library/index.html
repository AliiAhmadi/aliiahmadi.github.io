Books

A short description about books I have read.


  1. Calculus with Analytic Geometry — Richard A. Silverman (Volume 1, 2, 3)
    A foundational text in mathematics, this book offers a clear and rigorous introduction to calculus and analytic geometry. Silverman’s explanations build a strong conceptual understanding, essential for advanced studies in machine learning. Ideal for computer engineers.


  2. Bulletin of the Iranian Mathematical Society — Vol. 35 & 36, No. 2
    The Bulletin of the Iranian Mathematical Society is a biannual publication of the Iranian Mathematical Society in English. It publishes original research papers and invites survey articles in all area of mathematical sciences.


  3. Introduction to Graph Theory — Douglas B. West (Persian Translation)
    This book offers a clear and structured introduction to graph theory, covering both basic principles and more advanced topics. It explains key concepts such as trees, connectivity, planarity, coloring, and matching with mathematical precision and illustrative examples. Designed for students and readers with a background in mathematics, it balances theory with practical problem-solving and is widely recognized as a standard reference in the field.
